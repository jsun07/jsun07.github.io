I write in ink that shifts with time, some days bold, some days light— a river of thoughts, a quiet rhyme, a record of dark and bright. The page does not judge or blink, it simply holds the tide, of joy in gold, of gloom in ink, of moments I can’t hide. Tomorrow’s mood will spill anew, uncertain, raw, or free— but here my soul finds shape and hue, a self that speaks to me.
